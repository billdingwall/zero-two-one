# Product Requirements Document (PRD)

## 1. Problem Statement
**What is the user-facing problem we are solving?**
*Describe the pain point, gap, or opportunity from the user's perspective. Why does this need to be solved now?*

## 2. Target Audience
**Who are we building this for?**
*Identify the primary and secondary user personas. Be specific about their needs, behaviors, and constraints.*

## 3. Value Proposition
**What is the core benefit?**
*How does this feature/product improve the user's life or workflow? What makes our solution unique or better?*

## 4. Success Metrics
**How will we know we succeeded?**
*List clear, quantifiable metrics tied to outcomes (e.g., "Increase conversion by X%", "Reduce time-to-task by Y seconds"). Avoid vanity metrics.*

## 5. Scope Boundaries
**What is out of scope?**
*Explicitly state what we are NOT building in this iteration to prevent scope creep.*
